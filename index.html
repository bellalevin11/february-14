Dear Nick,

“One day at a time” — what you tell me time and time again when I think about the indefinite distance that will separate us for years to come.
“One day at a time” — what I tell my friends when they ask me what my plan is once you graduate.
“One day at a time” — what I tell myself during the endless weeks before I can finally see you again.

And you’re right. There’s really nothing else to do but take it day by day, loving each other through our screens, no matter how many miles separate us.

  • September 15 - December 19, 2023: 95 days
  • December 19, 2023 - August 26, 2024: 251 days
  • August 26 - December 18, 2024: 114 days
  • December 18, 2024 - February 14, 2025: 58 days

In 16 months of knowing and loving you, we’ve so far spent ~209 days together and ~309 days apart. 40% together, 60% apart, 100% confident in our relationship.

Long distance sucks - I miss you every day, and you’re (unfortunately) the easiest person to miss. The devil couldn’t reach me so he sent me the most perfect relationship with indefinite long distance.

We both come from crazy different places, lived different lives, come from different generations (boomer), and have had different experiences with love.

Before meeting you and falling in love with you against my own will (I was actively trying not to since someone was abandoning me), I was convinced I wouldn’t find that feeling again. That feeling of immediately clicking with another person, loving me as weird as I get, matching my energy (except once during drunk/high night), being able to spend countless hours with them and never wanting to leave. Little did I know, Basketball Nick “wya” was going to show me how easy it was to love him.

Thank God Valentine’s Day is on a Saturday in 2026 so I can (hopefully) properly spend it with my valentine.

I impatiently wait for the next time I can be in your arms. Happy Valentine's Day, my cute. I LOVE YOU.
